### Large Intestine

The large intestine is also called the colon. It is smaller in length in humans compared to the small intestine.

The colon has three parts:
*   Caecum
*   Colon
*   Rectum

The caecum joins the ileum with the colon.

The caecum is the receiving pouch for the waste matter.

The colon is home to many bacteria or intestinal flora that aid the digestion process.

The colon has four regions:
*   Ascending Colon
*   Transverse Colon
*   Descending Colon
*   Sigmoid Colon

The main functions of the colon are absorption of water and mineral salt from the undigested food (roughage) and to store waste materials.

The large intestine houses bacteria, such as *Escherichia coli* (*E. coli*), which are essential for life and use waste materials for synthesizing Vitamin B and K.

### Cellulose (19-03-2026)

Cellulose, the long chain carbohydrate characteristic of plant cell walls, reaches the large intestine undigested.
Although cellulose cannot be digested by humans, it serves as an important function, providing bulk.

As waste builds up in the large intestine, receptors in the walls of the intestine provide information to the central nervous system, which in turn promotes bowel movement.

A bowel movement ensures the removal of potentially toxic waste from the body.

Individuals who do not eat a sufficient amount of cellulose (roughage or fiber) have fewer bowel movements. This means the waste and toxins remain in their bodies for a longer period.

Individuals who eat mostly processed, highly refined food are more likely to develop cancer of the colon.

The rectum stores faeces until defecation.

The faeces are propelled using peristaltic movement during elimination.

The anus is an opening at the far end of the digestive tract and is the exit point for the waste material.

Two sphincters regulate the exit of faeces: the inner (involuntary) and the outer (voluntary).

**Bowel:** The long-tube shaped organ which completes the process of digestion.

### Bowel Movement

Bowel movement is the movement of faeces through the bowel and out of the anus.
