### Digestion in Mouth

There are both physical and chemical digestion of food.

*   **Physical digestion** occurs by chewing and grinding action of teeth.
*   **Chemical digestion** of starch (carbohydrate) starts by an enzyme called salivary amylase (ptyalin).

Maltose is the first product of digestion in the mouth.

The cavity inside the mouth consists of teeth and tongue and is called the buccal cavity.

Saliva, produced by the salivary gland, consists of:
*   Enzymes
*   Mucus
*   Water

Functions:
*   Water dissolves the food.
*   The mucus sticks digested food together.
*   Enzymes start the digestion of starch.

**Mastication:** Is the process of chewing food and mixing it with saliva with the help of the tongue and teeth.

**Bolus:** Is a ball-shaped swallowed mass of food from the mouth along the esophagus to the stomach.
