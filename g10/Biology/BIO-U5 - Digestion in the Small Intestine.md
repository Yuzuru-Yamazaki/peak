### Digestion in the Small Intestine (07-07-2018)

Most chemical digestion and absorption of digestive end products take place in the small intestine.

#### Parts of the Small Intestine

1.  **Duodenum:** The upper part of the small intestine, where the majority of chemical digestion takes place.
2.  **Jejunum:** Connects duodenum with ileum.
3.  **Ileum:** The lower part of the small intestine where digestion and absorption takes place.
#### The Accessory Organs of the Digestive System

The accessory organs are organs that add secretions and enzymes that break down food into nutrients.
The accessory organs include:
*   Salivary gland
*   Liver
*   Pancreas
*   Gallbladder

#### The Liver

The largest internal organ in humans, and it plays an important role in the digestion of fat and detoxification of blood.

*   The liver produces bile.
*   Bile is a digestive salt, not an enzyme.
*   It breaks down fat into fat droplets.
    Fat $\xrightarrow{\text{Bile (Emulsification)}}$ Fat droplets
*   Bile is temporarily stored in the gallbladder.
*   Bile neutralizes the acidity of chyme when the pyloric sphincter muscle opens.
*   Bile provides an alkaline or basic medium for the best functioning of pancreatic juice.
*   The characteristic brown color of faeces is the result of the color of bile.

#### The Pancreas

It is a tongue-like gland which lies beneath the stomach.

*   It secretes a juice called Pancreatic juice.
*   Pancreatic juice is secreted into the duodenum.

Pancreatic enzymes and their actions:
*   Starch $\xrightarrow{\text{Pancreatic Amylase}}$ Maltose
*   Fat droplets $\xrightarrow{\text{lipase}}$ Fatty acid + Glycerol
*   Peptone $\xrightarrow{\text{Trypsin}}$ Peptide

Trypsin is a protein-digesting enzyme. Trypsinogen is released from the pancreas. Once the trypsinogen reaches the small intestine, an enzyme called enterokinase converts the inactive trypsinogen into active trypsin.
Trypsinogen $\xrightarrow{\text{Enterokinase}}$ Trypsin
### Ileum Region (08-07-2018 EC)

The ileum region is the lower, the longest, and narrowest part of the small intestine.

*   It contains intestinal glands which secrete juice called intestinal juice.

Intestinal enzymes and their actions:
*   Maltose $\xrightarrow{\text{maltase}}$ Glucose + Glucose
*   Sucrose $\xrightarrow{\text{sucrase}}$ Glucose + Fructose
*   Lactose $\xrightarrow{\text{lactase}}$ Glucose + Galactose
*   Peptone $\xrightarrow{\text{Erepsin}}$ Amino Acids

### Digestive End Products

The digestive end products are:
*   Monomer units of carbohydrate
*   Amino acids
*   Fatty acids and Glycerol

### Absorption of Digested Food

Absorption is the process by which the end products of digestion pass from the walls of the small intestine and enter into the blood stream.

The absorptive structures are:
*   **Villi:** Absorbs monomer units of carbohydrate and amino acids.
*   **Lacteal:** Absorbs fatty acids and glycerol.
