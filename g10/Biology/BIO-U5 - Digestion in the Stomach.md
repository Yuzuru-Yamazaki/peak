### Digestion in the Stomach

The stomach is the site of food storage and initial protein digestion.

The stomach contains three layers of muscle which run in different directions, so that the muscular contraction can churn the food.

The movement of food into and from the stomach is regulated by a circular muscle called a sphincter.

The lower esophageal sphincter muscle prevents food and acid from being regurgitated up into the esophagus.

The pyloric sphincter muscle:
*   Regulates the movement of food and stomach acid into the small intestine.
*   Responsible for the contraction of stomach muscle.

#### Activities in the Stomach

The inner walls of the stomach have different activities:
1.  **Secretion** of stomach fluid called gastric juice that aids digestion.
2.  The **contraction and relaxation** of stomach muscle mix the food with gastric juice.
3.  It involves both physical and chemical digestion.
