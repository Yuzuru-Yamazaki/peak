Digestion is the process in which large, complex, insoluble food particles are changed into their smaller, simpler, and soluble form.

The human digestive system is composed of five digestive processes:

1.  **Ingestion:** Taking nutrients into the mouth.
2.  **Digestion**
3.  **Absorption:** The transport of digestive nutrients from the small intestine to the cells of the body through finger-like projections called villi.
4.  **Assimilation:** The ultimate use of digestive end products of food either for source of energy or to be incorporated into new parts of a cell.
5.  **Egestion:** The removal of food waste from the body.

### Distinction between Excretion and Egestion

The distinction between excretion and egestion is based on the types of waste excreted by an organism.

*   **Egestion:** The process of removing undigested food particles from the animal's body.
*   **Excretion:** The process of removing metabolic wastes from plants and animals.

### Digestive Tract / Alimentary Canal

The whole passage from mouth to anus:
Mouth $\rightarrow$ Esophagus $\rightarrow$ Stomach $\rightarrow$ Small Intestine $\rightarrow$ Large Intestine $\rightarrow$ Rectum $\rightarrow$ Anus
