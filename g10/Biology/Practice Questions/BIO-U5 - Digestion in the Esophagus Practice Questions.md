[[BIO-U5 - Digestion in the Esophagus]]
What type of digestion occurs in the esophagus?
A) Chemical digestion only
B) Physical digestion only
C) Both physical and chemical digestion
D) No digestion at all
Correct Answer: B
---
What is the term for the rhythmic contraction and relaxation of the esophageal muscle?
A) Mastication
B) Assimilation
C) Peristalsis
D) Egestion
Correct Answer: C
---
What is the primary function of peristalsis in the esophagus?
A) To chemically break down food
B) To absorb nutrients
C) To mix food with saliva
D) To push the food along
Correct Answer: D
---
Physical digestion in the esophagus is a result of the action of its:
A) Glands
B) Mucus
C) Muscles
D) Enzymes
Correct Answer: C
---
Peristalsis involves which two alternating actions?
A) Chewing and grinding
B) Secretion and absorption
C) Contraction and relaxation
D) Dissolving and binding
Correct Answer: C
---
Which of the following processes does NOT take place in the esophagus?
A) Physical digestion
B) Movement of food
C) Muscle contraction
D) Chemical digestion by enzymes
Correct Answer: D
---
Peristalsis is responsible for pushing food from the esophagus to which organ?
A) Mouth
B) Stomach
C) Small Intestine
D) Large Intestine
Correct Answer: B
---
The wave-like muscular movement described in the esophagus is known as:
A) Bolus formation
B) Mastication
C) Peristalsis
D) Salivation
Correct Answer: C
---
What causes food to move through the esophagus even when a person is upside down?
A) Gravity
B) The enzyme amylase
C) The action of peristalsis
D) The mucus in saliva
Correct Answer: C
---
The relaxation and contraction of the esophageal muscle is a form of:
A) Chemical breakdown
B) Physical digestion
C) Nutrient absorption
D) Enzymatic action
Correct Answer: B
---
