[[BIO-U5 - Digestion in the Mouth]]
What type of digestion in the mouth involves the chewing and grinding action of teeth?
A) Chemical digestion
B) Physical digestion
C) Enzymatic digestion
D) Hormonal digestion
Correct Answer: B
---
The chemical digestion of which nutrient begins in the mouth?
A) Protein
B) Fat
C) Starch
D) Fiber
Correct Answer: C
---
What is the name of the enzyme in saliva that starts carbohydrate digestion?
A) Pepsin
B) Lipase
C) Salivary amylase
D) Trypsin
Correct Answer: C
---
What is the first product of digestion that is formed in the mouth?
A) Glucose
B) Amino Acids
C) Maltose
D) Fatty Acids
Correct Answer: C
---
The cavity inside the mouth, which contains the teeth and tongue, is called the:
A) Pharyngeal cavity
B) Esophageal cavity
C) Gastric cavity
D) Buccal cavity
Correct Answer: D
---
Which of the following is NOT a component of saliva listed in the text?
A) Mucus
B) Water
C) Hydrochloric acid
D) Enzymes
Correct Answer: C
---
What is the function of water in saliva?
A) To start the digestion of starch
B) To dissolve the food
C) To stick digested food together
D) To kill bacteria
Correct Answer: B
---
The process of chewing food and mixing it with saliva is known as:
A) Peristalsis
B) Absorption
C) Mastication
D) Egestion
Correct Answer: C
---
What is a bolus?
A) A gland that produces saliva
B) An enzyme that breaks down starch
C) A ball-shaped swallowed mass of food
D) The action of chewing and grinding
Correct Answer: C
---
What is the alternative name for the enzyme salivary amylase?
A) Ptyalin
B) Pepsin
C) Maltase
D) Lipase
Correct Answer: A
---
What is the function of mucus in saliva?
A) To start the digestion of starch
B) To dissolve food particles
C) To stick digested food together
D) To regulate the mouth's pH
Correct Answer: C
---
Which structures are primarily responsible for mastication?
A) Esophagus and stomach
B) Salivary glands and pharynx
C) Tongue and teeth
D) Mucus and enzymes
Correct Answer: C
---
After a bolus is formed in the mouth, it travels along the esophagus to which organ?
A) Small Intestine
B) Stomach
C) Liver
D) Large Intestine
Correct Answer: B
---
The enzyme ptyalin acts specifically on what substance?
A) Proteins
B) Fats
C) Starch
D) Maltose
Correct Answer: C
---
Which two types of digestion both occur in the mouth?
A) Physical and hormonal
B) Chemical and cellular
C) Physical and chemical
D) Mechanical and emotional
Correct Answer: C
---
Saliva is produced by what structures?
A) The teeth
B) The tongue
C) The salivary glands
D) The buccal cavity walls
Correct Answer: C
---
Which component of saliva is responsible for initiating chemical digestion?
A) Water
B) Mucus
C) Enzymes
D) Bolus
Correct Answer: C
---
The primary purpose of forming a bolus is to:
A) Completely digest the food in the mouth
B) Prepare the food for swallowing
C) Absorb nutrients through the tongue
D) Sterilize the food before it enters the stomach
Correct Answer: B
---
In the mouth, physical digestion increases the surface area for which process to occur more efficiently?
A) Chemical digestion
B) Mastication
C) Bolus formation
D) Saliva production
Correct Answer: A
---
Which of these is the best description of the buccal cavity?
A) The ball of food ready to be swallowed
B) The enzyme that digests carbohydrates
C) The process of chewing
D) The space inside the mouth with teeth and a tongue
Correct Answer: D
---
What helps to combine chewed food into a single mass?
A) The grinding action of teeth
B) The water component of saliva
C) The mucus component of saliva
D) The enzyme ptyalin
Correct Answer: C
---
Which statement is true about digestion in the mouth?
A) Only physical digestion occurs.
B) Only chemical digestion of proteins occurs.
C) Both physical and chemical digestion of starch occur.
D) All food types are completely digested in the mouth.
Correct Answer: C
---
The tongue assists in which two major processes in the mouth?
A) Producing enzymes and dissolving food
B) Mastication and forming a bolus
C) Grinding food and producing mucus
D) Secreting saliva and digesting starch
Correct Answer: B
---
If salivary glands were unable to produce enzymes, which process would be directly affected?
A) The physical grinding of food
B) The chemical digestion of starch in the mouth
C) The dissolving of food by water
D) The formation of a bolus by mucus
Correct Answer: B
---
The term mastication refers specifically to the action of:
A) Swallowing
B) Secreting saliva
C) Chewing and mixing
D) Dissolving food
Correct Answer: C
---
The end goal of digestion in the mouth is to create a:
A) Pool of dissolved nutrients
B) Bolus
C) Micelle
D) Chyme
Correct Answer: B
---
Which part of saliva acts as a lubricant and binding agent?
A) Water
B) Ptyalin
C) Amylase
D) Mucus
Correct Answer: D
---
Where does the bolus travel immediately after leaving the mouth?
A) Into the stomach
B) Into the small intestine
C) Along the esophagus
D) Into the large intestine
Correct Answer: C
---
The action of salivary amylase on starch is an example of what?
A) Physical digestion
B) Mastication
C) Chemical digestion
D) Bolus formation
Correct Answer: C
---
All the digestive activities in the mouth work together to prepare food for which subsequent step?
A) Absorption
B) Swallowing
C) Assimilation
D) Egestion
Correct Answer: B
