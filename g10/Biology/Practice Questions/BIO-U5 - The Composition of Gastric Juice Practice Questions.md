[[BIO-U5 - The Composition of Gastric Juice]]
What is a primary function of hydrochloric acid (HCl) in the stomach?
A) To begin the digestion of fats.
B) To neutralize the stomach acid.
C) To kill pathogens from ingested food.
D) To protect the stomach lining.
Correct Answer: C
---
The enzyme pepsin is responsible for the hydrolysis of what substance?
A) Starch into maltose.
B) Protein into peptones.
C) Fats into fatty acids.
D) Milk protein into casein.
Correct Answer: B
---
What is the main purpose of mucus in the stomach?
A) To activate pepsinogen into pepsin.
B) To digest proteins into polypeptides.
C) To provide an acidic environment.
D) To protect the stomach wall from being digested.
Correct Answer: D
---
What is chyme?
A) The inactive form of the pepsin enzyme.
B) The coagulated milk protein in young mammals.
C) The soup-like paste of food that leaves the stomach.
D) The enzyme that stops the action of salivary amylase.
Correct Answer: C
---
How does hydrochloric acid affect enzymes in the stomach?
A) It activates salivary amylase and inactivates pepsin.
B) It neutralizes the function of salivary amylase and activates pepsinogen.
C) It activates both salivary amylase and pepsinogen.
D) It neutralizes both pepsin and ptyalin.
Correct Answer: B
---
What is the role of the renin enzyme in young mammals?
A) To hydrolyze protein into polypeptides.
B) To coagulate soluble milk protein.
C) To kill bacteria in ingested milk.
D) To protect the stomach lining from milk acid.
Correct Answer: B
---
What is the typical pH range inside the stomach?
A) 6.0 to 7.0 (Neutral)
B) 2.0 to 3.0 (Acidic)
C) 8.0 to 9.0 (Alkaline)
D) 4.5 to 5.5 (Slightly acidic)
Correct Answer: B
---
What is the main difference between a protein and a peptone?
A) The type of amino acids they contain.
B) The length of the amino acid chain.
C) Peptones are found in plants, proteins in animals.
D) One is soluble, the other is insoluble.
Correct Answer: B
---
Which component of gastric juice is responsible for converting inactive pepsinogen into active pepsin?
A) Renin
B) Mucus
C) Hydrochloric acid
D) Ptyalin
Correct Answer: C
---
What is the name given to the solidified, coagulated soluble milk protein?
A) Peptone
B) Pepsin
C) Casein
D) Polypeptide
Correct Answer: C
---
Which substance in the stomach denatures the ptyalin enzyme from the mouth?
A) Mucus
B) Pepsin
C) Renin
D) Hydrochloric acid
Correct Answer: D
---
The movement of chyme from the stomach is regulated by what structure?
A) The lower esophageal sphincter
B) The pyloric sphincter muscle
C) The stomach wall muscles
D) The mucus lining
Correct Answer: B
---
Why is an acidic medium necessary in the stomach?
A) For the best functioning of salivary amylase.
B) For the best functioning of protease enzymes like pepsin.
C) To help coagulate milk.
D) To protect the stomach wall.
Correct Answer: B
---
Which of the following is NOT a component of gastric juice listed in the text?
A) Hydrochloric acid
B) Mucus
C) Salivary amylase
D) Enzymes like pepsin
Correct Answer: C
---
The stomach wall is protected from being digested by protease enzymes primarily by:
A) Hydrochloric acid
B) Renin
C) Mucus
D) The pyloric sphincter
Correct Answer: C
---
When pepsin acts on protein, what is a resulting product?
A) Amino acids
B) Glucose
C) Polypeptide
D) Casein
Correct Answer: C
---
The acidic nature of the stomach, provided by HCl, serves as a defense mechanism by:
A) Neutralizing useful enzymes.
B) Coagulating proteins.
C) Killing pathogens like bacteria.
D) Denaturing the stomach wall.
Correct Answer: C
---
Renin is a specialized enzyme found in young mammals for digesting what specific food?
A) Meat
B) Starch
C) Milk
D) Plants
Correct Answer: C
---
In the reaction Protein + H₂O → Peptone, what is pepsin's role?
A) It is a reactant.
B) It is a product.
C) It is a catalyst.
D) It is an inactive enzyme.
Correct Answer: C
---
Chyme is best described as:
A) A solid mass of undigested food.
B) A clear, acidic fluid.
C) A coagulated milk solid.
D) A uniform to creamy paste.

Hydrochloric acid has a dual effect on enzymes: it inactivates ______ and helps activate ______.
A) pepsin; ptyalin
B) ptyalin; pepsinogen

Which enzyme catalyzes the hydrolysis of protein in the stomach?
