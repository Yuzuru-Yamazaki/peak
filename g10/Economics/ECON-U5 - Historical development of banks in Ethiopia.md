### A Historical Note

In Ethiopia modern banking started in 1905: with the establishment of Abyssinian Bank, based on a fifty-year agreement with the Anglo-Egyptian National Bank.

In 1908: a new development bank (called Société Nationale d'Ethiope Pour le Development de l'Agriculture et du Commerce) and two other foreign banks (Banque de l'Indochine and the Compagnie de l'Afrique Orientale) were also established.

These banks were criticized for being wholly foreign owned.
