### Angles in standard position
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/0GkmnPdD6jY" frameborder="0" allow="autoplay; encrypted-media" allowfullscreen></iframe>

An angle in the coordinate system is said to be in standard position if:
1.  Its vertex is at the origin.
2.  Its initial side lies on the positive x-axis.

#### Example 2

The following are measures of different angles. Put the angles in standard position.
a. $200^\circ$
b. $1125^\circ$
c. $-900^\circ$

**Solution:**
a. $200^\circ = 180^\circ + 20^\circ$
b. $1125^\circ = 3 \times 360^\circ + 45^\circ$
c. $-900^\circ = 2 \times (-360^\circ) + (-180^\circ)$
