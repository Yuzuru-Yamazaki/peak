## 4.2.2 Trigonometric values of angles
<iframe width="560" height="315" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/57VrEiEPD1I" frameborder="0" allow="autoplay; encrypted-media" allowfullscreen></iframe>

Trigonometry angles are the angles given by the ratios of the trigonometric functions. Trigonometry deals with the study of the relationship between angles and the sides of a triangle. The angle value ranges from $0^\circ$ to $360^\circ$. The important angles in trigonometry are $0^\circ, 30^\circ, 45^\circ, 60^\circ, 90^\circ, 180^\circ, 270^\circ$ and $360^\circ$. And the important six trigonometric ratios or functions are sine, cosine, tangent, cosecant, secant and cotangent.

Two angles are said to be **complementary angles** if they are added up to $90^\circ$. In Figure 4.21,
$60^\circ + 30^\circ = 90^\circ$. Hence, these two angles are complementary. Each angle of the complementary angles is called the "complement" of the other angle. Here, $60^\circ$ is the complement of $30^\circ$. Similarly, $30^\circ$ is the complement of $60^\circ$. Thus, the complement of an angle is calculated by subtracting the angle from $90^\circ$. So, the complementary angle of angle $x^\circ$ is $(90 - x)^\circ$.

#### Example 1

Find the complementary angle of $57^\circ$.

**Solution:**
The complementary angle of $57^\circ$ is obtained by subtracting it from $90^\circ$, that is,
$90^\circ - 57^\circ = 33^\circ$.
Thus, the complementary angle of $57^\circ$ is $33^\circ$.

#### Example 2

Evaluate the following angles:
a. $54^\circ$
b. $30^\circ$

**Solution:**
$\sin 54^\circ = \sin(90^\circ - 36^\circ) = \cos 36^\circ$, and $\sin 30^\circ = \sin(90^\circ - 60^\circ) = \cos 60^\circ$.

#### Note

Note the following on acute angle $\alpha$ and its complementary angle $(\pi/2 - \alpha)$.
*   $\sin (\pi/2 - \alpha) = \cos \alpha$
*   $\cos (\pi/2 - \alpha) = \sin \alpha$
*   $\tan (\pi/2 - \alpha) = \cot \alpha$
*   $\cot (\pi/2 - \alpha) = \tan \alpha$

#### Example 3

If $\sin 3A = \cos (A - 26^\circ)$, where $3A$ is an acute angle, find the value of A.

**Solution:**
Given that, $\sin 3A = \cos (A - 26^\circ)$ .... (1)
Since, $\sin 3A = \cos (90^\circ - 3A)$, we can write (1) as:
$\cos(90^\circ - 3A) = \cos (A - 26^\circ)$
$90^\circ - 3A = A - 26^\circ$
$90^\circ + 26^\circ = 3A + A$ which implies $4A = 116^\circ$.
So, $A = \frac{116^\circ}{4} = 29^\circ$.
Therefore, the value of A is $29^\circ$.
