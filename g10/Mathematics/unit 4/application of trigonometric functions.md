#### Example 1

From the top of a vertical cliff 40 m high, the angle of depression of an object that is at the level of the base of the cliff is $60^\circ$ as shown in Figure 4.39. How far is the object from the base of the cliff (A)?

**Solution:**
Let $x$ be the distance of the object in meters from the base of the cliff (A). The angle of depression is $60^\circ$. Here, $m(\angle APO) = m(\angle BOP)$ because they are alternate angles.
$\therefore m(\angle APO) = 60^\circ$.
From triangle APO, we have:
$\tan60^\circ = \frac{AO}{AP} = \frac{40}{x}$
which implies $x = \frac{40}{\tan 60^\circ} = \frac{40}{\sqrt{3}}$.
Therefore, the object is $\frac{40}{\sqrt{3}}$ m far from the cliff (A).

```
      O-----B (60°)
     /|
    / |
   /  | 40 m
  /   |
 /60° |
A-----P
   x

Figure 4.39
```

#### Example 2

A circus artist is climbing a 20 m long rope, which is tightly stretched and tied from the top of a vertical pole to the ground. Find the height of the pole if the angle is made by the rope with the ground level is $30^\circ$ (see Figure 4.41).

**Solution:**
In right angle $\triangle ABC$,
$\sin30^\circ = \frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{AB}{20}$
$\frac{1}{2} = \frac{AB}{20} \implies AB = \frac{1}{2} \times 20 = 10 \text{ m}$.
Therefore, the height of the pole is 10 m.

```
      A
     /|
    / |
   /  |
  /   | 20 m
 /30° |
C-----B

Figure 4.41
```

#### Example 3

A tree breaks due to storm and the broken part bends so that the top of the tree touches the ground making an angle $30^\circ$ with it. The distance between the foot of the tree to the point where the top touches the ground is 8 m. Find the original height of the tree before it breaks (see Figure 4.42).

**Solution:**
In right angle $\triangle ABC$,
$\tan30^\circ = \frac{AB}{BC} = \frac{AB}{8} \implies AB = 8 \tan30^\circ = \frac{8}{\sqrt{3}} \text{ m}$.
$\cos30^\circ = \frac{BC}{AC} = \frac{8}{AC} \implies AC = \frac{8}{\cos30^\circ} = \frac{8}{\sqrt{3}/2} = \frac{16}{\sqrt{3}} \text{ m}$.
Therefore, the height of the tree is $AB + AC = \frac{8}{\sqrt{3}} + \frac{16}{\sqrt{3}} = \frac{24}{\sqrt{3}} = 8\sqrt{3} \text{ m}$.

```
      A
     /|
    / |
   /  |
  /   |
 /30° |
C-----B
   8m

Figure 4.42
```
