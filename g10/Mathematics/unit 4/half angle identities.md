## Half angle identities

The half-angle identities for the sine and cosine are derived from two of the cosine identities described earlier.
From $\cos2\theta = 2\cos^2\theta - 1$, let $\theta = \frac{\alpha}{2}$, then $\cos\alpha = 2\cos^2\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) - 1$.
$2\cos^2\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) = \cos\alpha + 1$
$\cos^2\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) = \frac{1+\cos\alpha}{2}$. So, $\cos\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) = \pm\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos\alpha}{2}}$.

Similarly, from $\cos2\theta = 1 - 2\sin^2\theta$, let $\theta = \frac{\alpha}{2}$, then $\cos\alpha = 1 - 2\sin^2\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right)$.
$2\sin^2\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) = 1 - \cos\alpha$
$\sin^2\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) = \frac{1-\cos\alpha}{2}$. So, $\sin\left(\frac{\alpha}{2}\right) = \pm\sqrt{\frac{1-\cos\alpha}{2}}$.

The sign of the two preceding functions depends on the quadrant in which the resulting angle is located.

#### Example 1

Find the exact value for $\cos 15^\circ$ using the half-angle identity.

**Solution:**
$\cos15^\circ = \cos\left(\frac{30^\circ}{2}\right) = \pm\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos30^\circ}{2}}$
$= \sqrt{\frac{1+\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}}{2}}$ (Since $15^\circ$ is in the first quadrant, $\cos15^\circ > 0$)
$= \sqrt{\frac{2+\sqrt{3}}{4}} = \frac{\sqrt{2+\sqrt{3}}}{2}$.
